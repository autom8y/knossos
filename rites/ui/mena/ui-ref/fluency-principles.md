# Processing Fluency & First Impressions

> The cognitive mechanism behind aesthetic pleasure and the protocol for evaluating first impressions.

## Processing Fluency Theory (Reber et al. 2004)

Aesthetic pleasure is a function of processing fluency -- the ease with which a stimulus is perceived. Interfaces that are easier to process feel more beautiful. This is not metaphorical; it is the dominant mechanistic explanation for aesthetic preference in HCI.

**Fluency drivers** (each independently increases aesthetic pleasure):
- **Symmetry** -- Bilateral or approximate symmetry in layout
- **Figure-ground contrast** -- Clear separation between content and background
- **Prototypicality** -- Conformance to learned patterns for the interface category
- **Simplicity** -- Low visual complexity, few competing elements
- **Repetition** -- Consistent spacing, sizing, and visual rhythm

## The Inverted-U

Too fluent = too predictable = boring. A balance of typicality and novelty maximizes aesthetic evaluation. Default to high fluency, then introduce controlled novelty (a surprising color accent, an unconventional layout section, a distinctive illustration style) to avoid sterility.

## 50ms First-Impression Protocol (Lindgaard 2006)

Aesthetic judgments form in 50ms and are highly stable across longer exposures. Users do not "warm up" to a design -- first impressions persist.

**Implications**:
- Visual hierarchy must be clear at a glance; if primary content is not immediately findable, the aesthetic judgment is already formed
- Visual complexity negatively affects first impressions (Tuch et al. 2012)
- Prototypicality positively affects first impressions (Tuch et al. 2012)
- Both effects detectable within 50ms, with some evidence at 17ms

## First-Impression Checklist

When evaluating a design for first-impression quality, assess:

- [ ] **Visual hierarchy clear?** -- Primary content identifiable within the first viewport in under 2 seconds
- [ ] **Figure-ground unambiguous?** -- No regions where content blends into background
- [ ] **Layout parseable at a glance?** -- Structure communicates itself without reading
- [ ] **Novelty balanced with familiarity?** -- Something distinctive exists without confusing navigation or content discovery
- [ ] **Visual noise minimized?** -- No competing animations, decorative clutter, or unnecessary ornamentation
- [ ] **Prototypical for its category?** -- A dashboard looks like a dashboard; a landing page looks like a landing page
