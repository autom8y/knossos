# VisAWI Four-Facet Model

> Structured aesthetic evaluation using four empirically validated dimensions (Moshagen & Thielsch 2010).

## The Four Facets

### Simplicity
Layout clarity, visual noise, cognitive demand. Aligns with classical aesthetics (Lavie & Tractinsky 2004).

**Evaluate**:
- Is the structure immediately parseable? Can a user locate primary content within 2 seconds?
- Is visual noise minimized? Count competing elements in any viewport -- more than 5 focal points signals overload
- Does whitespace serve structure, or is it accidental?
- Are groupings clear without relying on decorative borders?

### Diversity
Creative variation, visual rhythm, engagement. Aligns with expressive aesthetics.

**Evaluate**:
- Does the design avoid monotony? Uniform grids with identical cards signal diversity deficit
- Is there purposeful variety without chaos? Layout breaks should serve communication goals
- Does visual rhythm (spacing, size alternation, typographic contrast) create engagement?
- Is novelty present without sacrificing parsability?

### Colorfulness
Color composition quality, palette harmony, accent effectiveness.

**Evaluate**:
- Is the palette cohesive? Test: can you describe it in 3-4 color roles (background, surface, primary, accent)?
- Do accents guide attention to the right elements? Track where eyes land first
- Is color usage semantically meaningful (not just decorative)?
- Does the palette hold across light/dark themes?

### Craftsmanship
Alignment precision, detail consistency, modernity of execution.

**Evaluate**:
- Are elements on a consistent spatial grid? Check for off-grid exceptions
- Are border radii, shadows, and spacing consistent across similar components?
- Does the typography scale feel intentional (clear hierarchy, consistent line heights)?
- Does the design feel contemporary and purposefully crafted, not dated or template-derived?

## Rating Scale

| Rating | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| **Strong** | Facet is well-executed; contributes positively to overall aesthetic impression |
| **Adequate** | Facet is acceptable; no obvious deficiencies but no standout quality |
| **Weak** | Facet has clear deficiencies that drag down overall aesthetic perception |

Rate each facet independently. A design can be Strong on simplicity but Weak on diversity -- the four facets are empirically distinct dimensions, not a single scale.
